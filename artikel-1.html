How to Keep Up with Your Team When They're On The Other Side of the Globe

Remote 'n Active

31. May, 2021

Reading Time 1 Min.

If you work with a remote team, you know what it's like to have a constant communication challenge. If you're in a global team, it's even more difficult. No matter how hard you work to stay in touch, it's tough to feel like you're on the same page. As a team leader, what's your strategy for managing your remote team?

Here are tips for remote teams that we've learned along the way.

Stay in touch with your remote team

Working remote does not mean losing the line. Which is why it is crucial to stay in touch with your team. These 4 things we've learned help make remote teams feel like they're in the same room as the rest of the team.

1. Align your goals

Most remote teams have some shared objectives. But unless you do a lot of focused planning, communication issues will arise. If you talk about things in the abstract, you'll end up talking past each other. Communication has to come with a goal in mind. This helps clarify the purpose of what you're communicating, and clarify what needs to be done. By sharing your goals, you'll all be in sync when you're speaking to each other. If the objective is e.g. to create a new video, you all know what that means. You can find out who has the most experience. You can come up with a plan for production. You can negotiate workflows that work for everybody.
E.g. by making use of templates, like this one from notion.

But what if the overall goal is not that clear? For example, the video "does not feel in line with the target audience" ?
Then you have to break the objectives into manageable chunks by tracking your goals.

2. Track your goals

A popular method to do this is the "OKR"-Method, or Objectives and Key Results , popularized by Google.
Get started with your OKR-planning with this notion template

3. Show your team you care

One of the main challenges of not sharing the same work space is that finished work can float away in a sea of not-noticingness. To prevent this it is very important that the team members know when you appreciate the work they have done. A simple Gif in a Slack Channel, or an uplifting compliment in the working documents will show your team that you see and care about their work.

"🎉👍 Great work with the OKR-Planning, Jean!"

via GIPHY


4. Talk about things not related to work

Just as you have casual conversations in the lift, or at the coffee machine about the weather, current sport events, or the newest hot topics from the yellow press, talk to your team about things not related to work once in a while. This lightens up the mood and supports you in getting a feeling of what are topics your team members care about.
All these activities might become a burden if you have to do them manually on a day-to-day basis. That is why you can make your life much easier by letting apps and tools take over some painful tasks.

How tools can help you

We have selected 5 tools for you which will help you engage with your team with ease.

1. Gatheraround

Gatheraround is a community building web service. Their mission is helping communities, companies, and teams feel a sense of togetherness and belonging, no matter where their people are.

Find it at Gatheraround

2. Crowd Party

Are your Zoom meetings so boring, that the participants are falling asleep? "Crowd Party App" is a website to play curated games over video calls, no registration required.

Find it at Crowd Party

3. Focus Mate

Stay productive and accountable with the help of a virtual team working session. Focus Mate helps you connect with a team member and boost productivity by cracking on tasks side-by-side.

Find it at Focus Mate

4. Tandem

Talking about virtual co-working: how about opening up a whole digital working space? You can do just that with the help of Tandem Chat, which lets you connect effortlessly with your distributed team.

Find it at Tandem Chat

5. Hallway

Let the app Hallway take care of your spontaneous water cooler chats. No more Excel sheets and meeting coordination, just install it for slack or Microsoft Teams.
Find it at Hallway

Conclusion

We've covered a lot of ground in this article. As a team leader you have to make sure everyone on the team is on the same page. Whether they are at the office, at home, or at the beach house drinking a cocktail. By aligning your goals and showing your team that you care about them, you will boost morale and give everyone the motivation and attention they need to fulfil their tasks to the best potential.
